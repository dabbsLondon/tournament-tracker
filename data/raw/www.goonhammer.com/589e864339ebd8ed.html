          Thoughts<\/h3>\n

          Ooo-kay this is going to be one of our “talk about why this is a mismatch” setups because this would go bad for the guard on pretty much anything except maybe Purge the Foe<\/strong>, and on Hammer and Anvil Supply drop it’s going to be an unmitigated disaster. The Grizzled list goes hard on Russes, creating a really broad setup of midweight threats that could potentially handle some foes by pulling them in too many directions. It will not achieve this here – the Emperor’s Children can spend their first turn staging up, and then the Guard will never score any Primary ever again. Hammer makes the objectives too condensed and the attack lines far too obvious for the Slaaneshi forces, and without Bullgryn to tie something up or Dorns to inflict\u00a0rapid<\/em> damage, there’s no real route to victory in the mission as it stands. Even on other missions, the Court Detachment Rule is really strong here, as it helps handle the number of 2+ saves that the EC can find themselves facing down in the current metagame, and being able to launch Maulerfiends through walls is a huge headache for a Guard build that’s low on screening. A fun time for Fulgrim, this one!<\/p>\n
