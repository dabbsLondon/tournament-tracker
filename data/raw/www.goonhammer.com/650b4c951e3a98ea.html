Each week our army list and tactics experts canvass the top tournaments for their game of specialty, looking at the week\u2019s top match-ups and how they played out on the tabletop to let you know what\u2019s hot, what\u2019s not, and what might be coming next as the competitive meta evolves year-round.<\/i><\/p>\n

Ah,\u00a0there<\/em> it is. Last week, Necrons were high up the win rates, but not converting it into top finishes. This tends to break one of two ways – either top players work out how to fend off the best builds, the wins don’t roll in, and player count drops\u00a0or<\/em> the builds grow more powerful as players get into the swing of using them, and they rise to dominance.<\/p>\n

Here we decidedly have the latter – Necrons are top of both the win rates and top finish stats as the full power of the C’tan is unleashed. That doesn’t massively surprise me from my own games with them – they’re so potent that they unlock entirely new lines of play, and it takes a while to fully get into the swing of that and maximise their power. At least I have a long trail of documentation that I liked C’tan before it was cool.<\/p>\n

Elsewhere, Marines (melee flavours and Blade) dominate the top placings, and Guard, T’au and Thousand Sons are nipping at the Necrons heels on win rate. Thousand Sons in particular have suddenly surged\u00a0massively<\/em> in both win rate and 4-1 finishes, suggesting they can handle the meta pretty well.\u00a0 Still plenty of stuff going on, but C’tan are definitely earning themselves a place on the “enjoy it while it lasts” list for the Necron fans of the world.<\/p>\n

          Thoughts<\/h3>\n

          Two very unusual builds here – Final Day in all its synergistic glory vs. a herohammer Hexwarp build that goes hard on Daemon Princes. The Hexwarp setup is super cool – what that detachment does well at its core is making one unit of Scarab Terminators very flexible, and Daemon Princes and Mutaliths are a great way to support that. The Daemon Princes take the combination of the Detachment Rule, Enhancements and their built-in Character-killing re-rolls and become very powerful roving threats, while Mutaliths are generically solid and provide a reliable anchor for everything else the army has going on.<\/p>\n

          Over on the Tyranid side, Final Day turns a big Patriarch\/Genestealer unit into an un-Overwatchable blender, pushes up the value of mining laser shooting across the board, and lets Hyperadapted Raveners really thrive – access to\u00a0Retreat Below<\/strong> means they have the best of both worlds. The list also gets some good cheap scoring in the Gargoyles, and one very powerful big volume ranged from the Neophytes.<\/p>\n

          In terms of who comes out on top here, I think both sides have some things going for them in this mission. The Thousand Sons list feels like it has the edge in a straight fight – the Genestealers are very far from harmless, but there are\u00a0lots<\/em> of threats on the Thousand Sons side that are competing for the attention of the big guns, whereas in the other direction the psychic threats can just murder whatever. However, the presence of the big Purestrain unit with re-roll access probably does force the Scarabs to be way more cautious than they’d like, which is an issue for an already slow unit, and the Cult can be quite muscular with the Purestrains given they have more than enough RP to spare to re-up the big squad. The Cult list is also better at playing Terraform – the Tsons list isn’t out and out\u00a0bad<\/em>, but their Tzaangors are pretty high up the Cults target priority, and they don’t want to be losing them early just to get the mission going.<\/p>\n

          I do still think this is either player’s game to win, and if I had to pick I’d probably give a narrow nod to the Tsons because of how deep their roster of big threats is (and how well they play into some targets, a Winged Daemon Prince can damn near solo a Hyperadapted unit with no help). The Cult were able to deploy their threats to counter this, however, and won the day.<\/p>\n
